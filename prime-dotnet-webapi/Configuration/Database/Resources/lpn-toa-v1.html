PharmaNet Licenced Practical Nurse Registrant Terms of Access

By enrolling for PharmaNet access, you agree to the following terms (the "Agreement"). Please read them carefully.

  1. BACKGROUND

    The Province owns and is responsible for the operation of PharmaNet, the province- wide network that links all health care providers in British Columbia to a central data system. Every prescription dispensed in community pharmacies is entered into PharmaNet.

    The purpose of providing you with access to PharmaNet is to enhance patient care by providing timely and relevant information to persons involved in the provision of direct patient care.

    PharmaNet contains highly sensitive confidential information, including Personal Information and the proprietary and confidential information of third- party licensors to the Province, and it is in the public interest to ensure that appropriate measures are in place to protect the confidentiality of all such information. All access to and use of PharmaNet and PharmaNet Data is subject to the Act and other applicable laws.

  2. INTERPRETATION

    1. Definitions. Unless otherwise provided, capitalized terms in these Terms of Access will have the meanings given below:

      • "Act" means the Pharmaceutical Services Act.

      • "Approved Site" means a location within which you provide Health Services and which is approved by the Province for PharmaNet access.

      • "Conformance Standards" means the following documents published by the Province, as amended from time to time:

        1. PharmaNet Professional and Software Conformance Standards

          https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/health/practitioner-professional-resources/software/conformance-standards: and

        2. Office of the Chief Information Officer: "Submission for Technical Security Standard and High Level Architecture for Wireless Local Area Network Connectivity".

          https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/health/practitioner-professional-resources/system-access/requirements-for-wireless-access-to-pharmanet.

      • "Health Services" means "health services" as defined in the Information Management Regulation.

      • "Information Management Regulation" means the Information Management Regulation, B.C. Reg. 328/2021.

      • "Personal Information" means recorded information about an identifiable individual.

      • "PharmaNet Access Agreement" means, in respect of an Approved Site, the "PharmaNet access agreement" (as defined in the Information Management Regulation) between the Province and the owner or operator of the Approved Site.

      • "PharmaCare Newsletter"means the PharmaCare newsletter published by the Province on the following website (or such other website as may be specified by the Province from time to time for this purpose): www.gov.bc.ca/pharmacarenewsletter

      • "PharmaNet" means PharmaNet as continued under section 2 of the Information Management Regulation.

      • "PharmaNet Data" includes any record or information contained in PharmaNet and any record or information obtained from PharmaNet that is in your custody, control or possession.

      • "PRIME" means the online service provided by the Province that allows users to apply for, and manage, their access to PharmaNet, and through which users are granted access by the Province.

      • "Province" means His Majesty the King in Right of British Columbia, as represented by the Minister of Health.

      • "Professional College" means the regulatory body governing your provision of Health Services.

    2. Reference to Enactments. In these Terms of Access, a reference to a statute or regulation by name means the statute or regulation of British Columbia of that name, as amended or replaced from time to time, and includes any enactment made under the authority of that statute or regulation.

  3. ACCESS TERMS

    1. Acknowledgement. You acknowledge that:

      1. the Act and the Information Management Regulation govern your PharmaNet access;
      2. PharmaNet Data accessed or obtained as a result of such access is disclosed to you by the Province under the authority of the Act;
      3. sections 24 and 25(3) of the Act govern your further use or disclosure of PharmaNet Data; and
      4. these terms of access document limits and conditions set, in writing, by the minister, that you are required to comply with pursuant to section 24(2) of the Act.

    2. Compliance with Applicable Law. You must comply with the Act, the Information Management Regulation and all other laws applicable to PharmaNet access and PharmaNet Data.

    3. Written Limits and Conditions of Access. The following limits and conditions apply to your access to PharmaNet:

      1. unless (ii) below applies, you must only access PharmaNet when you are physically at an Approved Site, using the technologies and applications at the Approved Site that are approved by the Province for use for PharmaNet access.
      2. you may access PharmaNet "remotely" and when you are not physically at the Approved Site only if all the following conditions are met:
        1. the owner or operator of the Approved Site has registered you with the Province for remote access through the Approved Site;
        2. you have applied for remote access at the Approved Site using PRIME and are approved by the Province;
        3. you are physically located in British Columbia at the time of any such remote access:
        4. the remote access technology used at the Approved Site has been specifically approved in writing by the Province, and
        5. the requirements of the Province’s Policy for Remote Access to PharmaNet (https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/health/practitioner- professional-resources/software/conformance-standards) are met.
      3. you will complete any PharmaNet-related training programs made available to you by the software support organization that provides the information technology software and/or services for accessing PharmaNet at the Approved Site;
      4. you acknowledge that the Province may, in writing, communicate additional written limits or conditions that apply to your access to PharmaNet, in which case you must comply with any such limits or conditions in addition to the limits and conditions established under these terms of access.
      5. you must not permit on-behalf-of users (as defined in the Information Management Regulation) to access PharmaNet on your behalf.

    4. Privacy and Security Measures. You will take all reasonable measures to safeguard Personal Information, including any Personal Information in the PharmaNet Data, while it is in your custody, control or possession. Without limiting the foregoing, you will:

      1. secure any workstations used to access PharmaNet and all devices, codes or passwords that enable access to PharmaNet;
      2. take such other privacy and security measures as the Province may reasonably require from time-to-time.

    5. Conformance Standards. You will comply with the rules specified in the Conformance Standards when accessing and recording information in PharmaNet.

  4. DISCLOSURE, STORAGE, AND ACCESS REQUESTS

    1. Retention of PharmaNet Data. You will not store or retain PharmaNet Data in any paper files or any electronic system, unless such storage or retention is required for record keeping in connection with your provisions of Health Services; is in accordance with the requirements of your Professional College; and is otherwise in compliance with the Conformance Standards. You will not modify any records retained in accordance with this section other than as may be expressly authorized in the Conformance Standards. For clarity, you may annotate a discrete record provided that the discrete record is not itself modified other than as expressly authorized in the Conformance Standards.

    2. Use of Retained Records. You may use any records retained by you in accordance with section 4(a) of these Terms of Access for a purpose authorized under section 24(1) of the Act.

    3. Secondary Use. Subject to section 4(b) of these Terms of Access, you must not use PharmaNet Data for the purpose of quality improvement, evaluation, health care planning, surveillance, research, or any purpose other than your provision of Health Services.

    4. Disclosure to Third Parties. You must not disclose PharmaNet Data to any third party unless such disclosure is required for your provision of Health Services or is otherwise authorized under section 24(1) of the Act.

    5. Responding to Patient Access Requests. Aside from any records retained by you in accordance with section 4(a) of these Terms of Access, you will not provide to patients any copies of records containing PharmaNet Data or "print outs" produced directly from PharmaNet, and will refer any requests for access to such records or "print outs" to the Province.

    6. Responding to Requests to Correct a Record contained in PharmaNet. If you receive a request for correction of any record or information contained in PharmaNet, you will refer the request to the Province.

    7. Legal Demands for Records Contained in PharmaNet. You will immediately notify the Province if you receive any order, demand or request compelling, or threatening to compel, disclosure of records contained in PharmaNet. You will cooperate and consult with the Province in responding to any such demands. For greater certainty, the foregoing requires that you notify the Province only with respect to any access requests or demands for records contained in PharmaNet, and not records retained by you in accordance with section 4(a) of these Terms of Access.

  5. ACCURACY

    You will make reasonable efforts to ensure that any Personal Information recorded by you in PharmaNet is accurate, complete and up to date. In the event that you become aware of a material inaccuracy or error in such information, you will take reasonable steps to investigate the inaccuracy or error, correct it if necessary, and notify the Province of the inaccuracy or error and any steps taken.

  6. NOTICE OF NON COMPLIANCE

    1. Non-Compliance. You will promptly notify the Province, and provide particulars, if:
      1. you do not comply, or you anticipate that you will be unable to comply with these Terms of Access in any respect, or
      2. you have knowledge of any circumstances, incidents or events which have or may jeopardize the security, confidentiality, or integrity of PharmaNet, the provincial drug program, or any government network or electronic system including any unauthorized attempt, by any person, to access PharmaNet.

    2. Reports to College or Privacy Commissioner. You acknowledge that the Province may report of any material breach of the Act, the Information Management Regulation, or these Terms of Access to your Professional College or to the Information and Privacy Commissioner of British Columbia.

  7. SUSPENSION & TERMINATION OF ACCESS

    1. Termination by You for Any Reason. You may terminate your grant of access to PharmaNet at any time on written notice to the Province.

    2. Termination of PharmaNet access. The Province may take administrative action against you in relation to your grant of access to PharmaNet, including to terminate or suspend your access, in accordance with the provisions of the Information Management Regulation.

    3. Suspension of Account for Inactivity. As a security precaution, the Province may suspend your account after a period of inactivity, in accordance with the Province’s policies. Please contact the Province immediately if your account has been suspended for inactivity but you still require access to PharmaNet.
