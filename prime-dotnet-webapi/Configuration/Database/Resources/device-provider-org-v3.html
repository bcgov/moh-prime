ORGANIZATION PHARMANET ACCESS AGREEMENT

This Organization PharmaNet Access Agreement (the "Agreement") is executed as of the date first written above, by {{organization_name}} ("Organization") for the benefit of HIS MAJESTY THE KING IN RIGHT OF THE PROVINCE OF BRITISH COLUMBIA, as represented by the Minister of Health (the "Province").

WHEREAS:

  1. The Province owns and is responsible for the operation of PharmaNet, the province-wide network that links health care providers to a central data system. Every prescription dispensed in community pharmacies in British Columbia is entered into PharmaNet.
  2. PharmaNet contains highly sensitive confidential information, including personal information, and it is in the public interest to ensure that appropriate measures are in place to protect the confidentiality and integrity of such information. All access to and use of PharmaNet and PharmaNet Data is subject to the Act and other applicable law.
  3. The Province permits authorized users to access PharmaNet to provide health services to the individual whose personal information is being accessed.
  4. This Agreement sets out the terms by which Organization may permit its Authorized Staff to access PharmaNet in connection with the Site(s) operated by Organization.

NOW THEREFORE Organization makes this Agreement knowing that the Province will rely on it in permitting access to and use of PharmaNet from the Site(s) operated by Organization. Organization conclusively acknowledges that reliance by the Province on this Agreement is in every respect justifiable and that it received fair and valuable consideration for this Agreement, the receipt and adequacy of which is hereby acknowledged. Organization hereby agrees as follows:

ARTICLE 1 – INTERPRETATION

    1. 1.1 In this Agreement, terms that are used but not defined will have the same meaning as in the Information Management Regulation. The following capitalized terms will have the meaning given below:
      1. "Act" means the Pharmaceutical Services Act;
      2. "Approved" means approved in writing by an official in the Province’s Pharmaceutical, Laboratory and Blood Services Division, and "Approval" will have the same meaning;
      3. "Approved SSO" means, in relation to a Site, the software support organization identified in the Site Registration as providing Organization with the SSO-Provided Technology used at the Site;
      4. "Associated Technology" means, in relation to a Site, the information technology hardware, software or services used at the Site, other than the SSO-Provided Technology, in connection with PharmaNet Access or to store or transmit PharmaNet Data;

      5. "Authorized Staff" means any individual granted access to PharmaNet by the Province who:

        1. is an employee or independent contractor of Organization,
        2. if Organization operates a hospital as defined in section 1 of the Hospital Act, is a member of the medical staff of the hospital, or
        3. if Organization is an individual, is the Organization;
      6. "Conformance Standards" means the PharmaNet Professional and Software Conformance Standards ( https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/health/practitioner-professional-resources/software/conformance-standards) as published by the Province from time to time;
      7. "Control" means control of greater than fifty percent of the voting rights or equity interests in Organization;
      8. "Device Provider Staff" means Authorized Staff that are "device provider agents" under the Information Management Regulation;
      9. "Information Management Regulation" means the Information Management Regulation, B.C. Reg. 328/2021;
      10. "On-Behalf-Of Staff" means Authorized Staff that are "on-behalf-of users" under the Information Management Regulation;
      11. "PharmaNet Access" includes Site Access;
      12. "PharmaNet Data" includes any record or information contained in PharmaNet and any record or information obtained from PharmaNet that is in the custody, control or possession of Organization or any of its employees, independent contractors or staff;
      13. "PRIME" means the online service provided by the Province for the purposes of (i) Authorized Staff members being granted access to PharmaNet by the Province, and (ii) Organization’s Site Registration. Where this Agreement refers to submission of information "through PRIME", that includes the use of any paper-based or other alternate mechanism provided by the Province for the submission of such information;
      14. "Registrant Staff" means Authorized Staff that are "registrants" under the Information Management Regulation;
      15. "Signing Authority" means the individual identified as the "Signing Authority" in the Site Registration for a Site;
      16. "Site" means a premises located in British Columbia that is operated by Organization and Approved pursuant to section 3.1;
      17. "Site Access" means any access to PharmaNet by an individual when the individual is at a Site;
      18. "Site Registration" means the "site registration" submitted by Organization to the Province through PRIME, which includes all information required by the Province, all as updated from time to time by Organization in accordance with section 5.2 of this Agreement;
      19. "SSO-Provided Technology" means any information technology hardware, software or services provided to Organization by an Approved SSO for the purpose of Site Access.
    2. Unless otherwise specified, a reference to a statute or regulation by name means a statute or regulation of British Columbia of that name, as amended or replaced from time to time, and includes any enactments made under the authority of that statute or regulation.
    3. The following is the Schedule attached to and incorporated into this Agreement:
      • Schedule A – Specific Privacy and Security Measures
      • Schedule B – On-Behalf- Staff Site Access Exceptions
    4. The main body of this Agreement, the Schedule, and any documents incorporated by reference into this Agreement are to be interpreted so that all of the provisions are given as full effect as possible. In the event of a conflict, unless expressly stated to the contrary the main body of the Agreement will prevail over the Schedule, which will prevail over any document incorporated by reference.

        1. Organization has the power and capacity to enter into this Agreement and to comply with its terms;
        2. all necessary corporate or other proceedings have been taken to authorize the execution and delivery of this Agreement by, or on behalf of, Organization; and
        3. this Agreement has been legally and properly executed by, or on behalf of, the Organization and is legally binding upon and enforceable against Organization in accordance with its terms.

ARTICLE 3 – SITE ACCESS REQUIREMENTS

    1. Organization must submit a Site Registration to the Province for each physical location where it intends to provide Site Access.
    2. Organization must only provide Site Access if the Site Registration for the Site has been Approved by the Province.
    3. Organization must only provide Site Access using SSO-Provided Technology. Such SSO-Provided Technology must comply with the Conformance Standards and otherwise meet the version and currency requirements of the Province.
    4. Unless otherwise Approved by the Province, Organization must at all times use the secure network or security technology that the Province certifies or makes available to Organization for the purpose of Site Access. The use of any such network or technology by Organization may be subject to terms and conditions of use, including acceptable use policies, established by the Province and communicated to Organization from time to time in writing.
    5. Organization must only allow Site Access by the following users:
      1. Authorized Staff when they are physically located at a Site, and
      2. Representatives of an Approved SSO for technical support purposes, in accordance with section 15 of the Information Management Regulation.
    6. Except as may be expressly authorized in Schedule B (On-Behalf-Of Staff Site Access Exceptions), Organization must ensure that On-Behalf-Of Staff users only access PharmaNet from the Site where the Registrant Staff or Device Provider Staff user that they are accessing for directly provided health services to the person in respect of whom PharmaNet is being accessed.

ARTICLE 5 – GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

    1. Organization must comply with the Act and all applicable law.
    2. Organization must immediately notify the Province:
      1. if a Site, or any portion thereof, is leased or transferred to another person;
      2. if Organization ceases to operate a Site;
      3. if Organization acquires a premises that already has PharmaNet access, whether or not Organization intends to provide Site Access from that location;
      4. of any change to the information contained in a Site Registration, including any change to the Site’s status, location, normal operating hours, Approved SSO, or the name and contact information of the Signing Authority or any of the other specific roles set out in the Site Registration; and
      5. of a change of Control of Organization.
    3. Organization must ensure that Authorized Staff:
      1. only access PharmaNet to the extent necessary to provide health services to the individual whose personal information is being accessed;
      2. only submit claims through PharmaNet if the Site is enrolled as a provider under the Provider Regulation, B.C. Reg. 76/2022;
      3. first complete any mandatory training program(s) that the Site’s Approved SSO or the Province makes available in relation to PharmaNet;
      4. access PharmaNet using their own separate login identifications and credentials, and do not share or have multiple use of any such login identifications and credentials;
      5. secure all devices, codes and credentials that enable access to PharmaNet against unauthorized use;
      6. are correctly identified as required by the Province on all transactions submitted to PharmaNet, including "batch jobs" or similar;
      7. only use PharmaNet in accordance with the most current PharmaCare Policy Manual.
    4. If notified by the Province that an Authorized Staff user’s PharmaNet Access has been suspended or revoked by the Province, Organization will immediately take any local measures necessary to remove that individual’s PharmaNet Access. Organization will only restore PharmaNet Access to a previously suspended or revoked Authorized Staff user upon the Province’s specific written direction.
    5. The PharmaNet Data disclosed pursuant to this Agreement is disclosed by the Province solely for the Use of the Registrant Staff or Device Provider Staff user to whom it is disclosed (or on whose behalf it was obtained by an On-Behalf-Of Staff user).
      Organization must not Use any PharmaNet Data, or permit any third party to Use PharmaNet Data, for Organization’s (or the third party’s) own purposes, except as necessary for the Registrant Staff or Device Provider Staff user’s provision of health services.
      For the purposes of this section, "Use" includes to collect, access, retain, use, process, link, de-identify, modify or disclose.
    6. Organization must make reasonable arrangements to protect PharmaNet Data against such risks as unauthorized access, collection, use, modification, retention, disclosure or disposal, including by:
      1. taking all reasonable physical, technical and operational measures necessary at Organization’s Site(s) to ensure PharmaNet Access operate in accordance with the requirements of Articles 3, 4 and 5 of this Agreement, and
      2. complying with the requirements of Schedule A (Specific Privacy and Security Measures).

ARTICLE 6 – NON-COMPLIANCE AND INVESTIGATIONS

    1. Organization must promptly notify the Province, and provide particulars, if Organization does not comply, or anticipates that it will be unable to comply, with the terms of this Agreement, or if Organization has knowledge of any circumstances, incidents or events which have or may jeopardize the security, confidentiality or integrity of PharmaNet, including any attempt by any person to gain unauthorized access to PharmaNet or the networks or equipment used to connect to PharmaNet or convey PharmaNet Data.
    2. Organization must immediately investigate any suspected breaches of this Agreement and take all reasonable steps to prevent recurrences of any such breaches.
    3. Organization must cooperate with any audits or investigations conducted by the Province (including any independent auditor appointed by the Province) regarding compliance with this Agreement, including by providing access upon request to a Site and any associated facilities, networks, equipment, systems, books, records and personnel for the purposes of such audit or investigation.

ARTICLE 7 – SITE TERMINATION

    1. Unless otherwise Approved by the Province, Organization must take all measures necessary to prevent further PharmaNet Access from a Site, or any portion thereof, that is leased or transferred to another person, or otherwise ceases to be operated by Organization.
    2. The Province may change the type of PharmaNet Access it provides to a Site at any time and in its sole discretion and without notice to any person.
    3. The Province may terminate all PharmaNet Access at a Site immediately, upon notice to the Signing Authority for the Site, if:
      1. the Approved SSO for the Site is no longer approved by the Province to provide information technology hardware, software, or service in connection with PharmaNet, or
      2. the Province determines that the SSO-Provided Technology or Associated Technology in use at the Site, or any component thereof, is obsolete, unsupported, or otherwise poses an unacceptable security risk,
      and the Organization is unable or unwilling to remedy the problem within a timeframe acceptable to the Province.
    4. As a security precaution, the Province may suspend PharmaNet Access at a Site after a period of inactivity. If PharmaNet Access at a Site remains inactive for a period of 90 days or more, the Province may, immediately upon notice to the Signing Authority for the Site, terminate all further PharmaNet Access at the Site.
    5. Organization must take all measures necessary to prevent further PharmaNet Access at a Site immediately upon the Signing Authority’s receipt of notice from the Province under sections 7.3 or 7.4.

ARTICLE 8 – TERM AND TERMINATION

    1. The term of this Agreement begins on the date first noted above and continues until it is terminated in accordance with this Article 8.
    2. Organization may terminate this Agreement at any time on written notice to the Province.
    3. The Province may terminate this Agreement immediately upon notice to Organization if:
      1. the Province determines that Organization has failed to comply with any provision of this Agreement;
      2. Organization no longer operates any Site from which PharmaNet Access is permitted by the Province; or
      3. there is a change of Control of Organization without the Province’s Approval.
    4. In addition to the Province’s right to terminate under section 8.3, the Province may terminate this Agreement for any reason upon two (2) months advance notice to Organization.
    5. Organization must prevent any further PharmaNet Access immediately upon termination of this Agreement.

SCHEDULE A – SPECIFIC PRIVACY AND SECURITY MEASURES

Organization must, in relation to each Site and in relation to PharmaNet Access:

  1. secure all workstations and printers at the Site to prevent any viewing of PharmaNet Data by persons other than Authorized Staff;

  2. implement all privacy and security measures specified in the following documents published by the Province, as amended from time to time:

    1. the Conformance Standards

      ( https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/health/practitioner-professional-resources/software/conformance-standards )
  3. ensure that a qualified technical support person is engaged to provide security support for the Site. This person should be familiar with the Site’s network configurations, hardware and software, including all SSO-Provided Technology and Associated Technology, and should be capable of understanding and adhering to the standards set forth in this Agreement and Schedule. Note that any such qualified technical support person must not be permitted by Organization to access or use PharmaNet in any manner, except as expressly permitted under this Agreement;
  4. establish and maintain documented privacy policies that detail how Organization will meet its privacy obligations in relation to the Site;
  5. establish breach reporting and response processes in relation to the Site;
  6. detail expectations for privacy protection in contracts and service agreements as applicable at the Site;
  7. regularly review the administrative, physical and technological safeguards at the Site;
  8. establish and maintain a program for monitoring PharmaNet Access, including by making appropriate monitoring and reporting mechanisms available to Authorized Staff for this purpose.

SCHEDULE B – ON-BEHALF- STAFF SITE ACCESS EXCEPTIONS

On-Behalf-Of Staff acting on behalf of a Device Provider Staff user may access PharmaNet at a Site that is different from the one where the Device Provider Staff user provided health services to the person in respect of whom PharmaNet is being accessed only if that different Site is registered by the Device Provider Staff user for the purpose of their submission of claims as permitted under the Provider Regulation.
