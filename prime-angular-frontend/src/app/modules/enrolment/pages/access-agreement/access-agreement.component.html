COMMUNITY HEALTH PRACTITIONER PHARMANET ACCESS AGREEMENT

1. Background

The Province owns and is responsible for the operation of an electronic health information system known as PharmaNet. PharmaNet is the province-wide network that links B.C. community pharmacies to a central data system. Every prescription dispensed in community pharmacies in B.C. is entered into PharmaNet.

The purpose of providing you, and the Supervised Persons whom you have authorized, with access to PharmaNet is to enhance patient care by providing timely and relevant information to persons involved in the provision of care to patients.

PharmaNet contains highly sensitive confidential information, including Personal Information and the proprietary and confidential information of third party licensors to the Province, and it is in the public interest to ensure that appropriate measures are in place to protect the confidentiality of all such information. All access to and use of PharmaNet and data within PharmaNet Data is subject to the Act and Privacy Laws.

You are accountable for your and Supervised Persons’ compliance with the terms and conditions of this Agreement and all policies, guidelines, and directives applicable to PharmaNet.

2. INTERPRETATION

    • “Act” means the Pharmaceutical Services Act.

    • “Approved SSO” means a software support organization approved by the Province that provides you with the information technology software and/or services through which you and Supervised Persons access PharmaNet.

    • “Conformance Standards” means the following documents published by the Province, as amended from time to time:

      1. PharmaNet Professional and Software Compliance Standards; and
      2. Office of the Chief Information Officer: “Submission for Technical Security Standard and High Level Architecture for Wireless Local Area Network Connectivity”.

    • “Direct Patient Care” means the health care services you provide to a specific individual in your Practice.

    • “Information Management Regulation” means the Information Management Regulation, B.C. Reg. 74/2015, as amended.

    • “Personal Information” means all recorded information that is about an identifiable individual or is defined as, or deemed to be, “personal information” or “personal health information” pursuant to any Privacy Laws.

    • “PharmaNet” means PharmaNet as continued under section 2 of the Information Management Regulation.

    • “PharmaNet Data” includes any record or information contained in PharmaNet and any record or information in the custody, control or possession of you or a Supervised Person that was obtained through your or a Supervised Person’s access to PharmaNet.

    • “Practice” means your practice of the health profession regulated by the Health Professions Act and governed by the regulatory body identified on page 1 of this Agreement.

    • “Privacy Laws” means the Act, the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act, the Personal Information Protection Act, and any other statutory or legal obligations of privacy owed by you or the Province, whether arising under statute, by contract or at common law.

    • “Professional College” is the regulatory body governing the Practice you have identified on page 1 of this Agreement.

    • “Schedule” means the “Further Limits or Conditions” Schedule attached to this Agreement, which is incorporated into this Agreement by reference and deemed to be an integral part of this Agreement.

    • “Supervised Person” means a member of your staff who: (i) requires access to PharmaNet to carry out Direct Patient Care duties in relation to your Practice; and (ii) is authorized by you to access PharmaNet on your behalf.

    • “Unauthorized Person” means any person other than:

      1. you,
      2. a Supervised Person, or
      3. a representative of an Approved SSO that is accessing PharmaNet for technical support purposes in accordance with section 6 of the Information Management Regulation.

5. ACCESS
  1. Grant of Access. The Province will provide you with access to PharmaNet subject to your compliance with the limits and conditions set out in section 5(b) below and otherwise in this Agreement. The Province may from time to time, at its discretion, amend or change the scope of your access privileges to PharmaNet as privacy, security, business and clinical practice requirements change. In such circumstances, the Province will use reasonable efforts to notify you of such changes.
  2. Limits and Conditions Of Access. You represent and warrant that:
    1. you are a registrant in good standing with the Professional College and your licence permits you to deliver direct patient care ;
    2. you will only access PharmaNet, and you will ensure that Supervised Persons only access PharmaNet, from the place(s) of practice within British Columbia that you have identified to the Province on form HLTH 4530, or the amendment form HLTH 7092;
    3. you will notify the Province at least 10 business days in advance of any transfer or wind up your Practice, or of any change to your business address from that which you have identified to the Province on form HLTH 4530 or the amendment form HLTH 7092;
    4. you will only access PharmaNet as necessary for your provision of Direct Patient Care, and you will ensure that Supervised Persons only access PharmaNet as necessary to support your provision of Direct Patient Care;
    5. you will not under any circumstances access PharmaNet, or use PharmaNet Data, for the purpose of market research, and you will ensure that no Supervised Persons access PharmaNet, or use PharmaNet Data, for the purpose of market research;
    6. subject to section 6(b) of this Agreement, you will not use PharmaNet Data, and you will ensure that Supervised Persons do not use PharmaNet Data, for any purpose other than your provision of Direct Patient Care, including for the purposes of quality improvement, evaluation, health care planning, surveillance, research or other secondary uses;
    7. you will not permit any Unauthorized Person to access PharmaNet, and you will take all reasonable measures to ensure that no Unauthorized Person can access PharmaNet;
    8. you will immediately notify the Province of any need to suspend or terminate a Supervised Person’s access to PharmaNet, including where the Supervised Person ceases to be one of your staff or has begun a leave of absence from his/her position as a member of your staff, or where the Supervised Person’s duties in relation to the Practice have changed;
    9. you will ensure that every Supervised Person first executes a confidentiality undertaking on form HLTH 4531 before accessing PharmaNet, and will retain (and provide to the Province upon the Province’s request) all such executed forms for the duration of the term of this Agreement and for four (4) years thereafter;
    10. you will complete any training program(s) that your Approved SSO makes available to you in relation to PharmaNet, and you will ensure that all Supervised Persons complete such training;
    11. you will comply with, and you will ensure that Supervised Persons comply with, any further limits or conditions applicable to you, as set forth in the Schedule or otherwise in writing by the Province.
  3. Responsibility for Supervised Persons: you agree that you are responsible under this Agreement for all activities undertaken by Supervised Persons in relation to their access to PharmaNet and use of PharmaNet Data.
  4. Privacy and Security Measures. You are responsible for taking all reasonable measures to safeguard Personal Information, including any Personal Information in the PharmaNet Data while it is in the custody, control or possession of yourself or a Supervised Person. You will ensure that any workstations used to access PharmaNet, and all devices, codes or passwords that enable access to PharmaNet by yourself or Supervised Persons are secure. In particular, you will:
    1. take all reasonable steps to ensure the physical security of Personal Information, generally and as required by Privacy Laws;
    2. secure all workstations and printers in a protected area in the Practice to prevent viewing of PharmaNet Data by Unauthorized Persons;
    3. ensure separate identification for each Supervised Person, and prohibit sharing or other multiple use of your identification and password, or a Supervised Person’s identification and password, for access to PharmaNet;
    4. take reasonable security steps to protect all codes or passwords that enable access to PharmaNet, including without limitation, user identifications, hardware or software tokens, and other security devices or certificates;
    5. implement all technical security requirements for connection to PharmaNet as directed by the Province in writing, including by only accessing PharmaNet using:
      1. the PharmaNet compliant software and services of an Approved SSO, and
      2. the secure network or security technology that the Province certifies or makes available to you for the purpose of connecting to PharmaNet, such as “HNSecure” or any replacement or additional secure network or security technology;
    6. monitor and ensure appropriate access by Supervised Persons to PharmaNet in accordance with this Agreement, including by regularly reconciling logs of PharmaNet access by yourself and all Supervised Persons against patient visits; and
    7. such other privacy and security measures as the Province may reasonably require from time-to-time.

    The use of any secure network or security technology of the Province referred to in paragraph (vi) above is subject to terms and conditions of use, including acceptable use policies, established by the Province and communicated to you from time to time in writing.

  5. Conformance Standards - Business Rules. You will comply with, and will ensure Supervised Persons comply with, the business rules specified in the Conformance Standards when accessing and recording information in PharmaNet.
6. DISCLOSURE, STORAGE, AND ACCESS REQUESTS
  1. Retention of PharmaNet Data. You will not store or retain PharmaNet Data in your own paper files or any electronic system, unless such storage or retention is required for record keeping in accordance with Professional College requirements and in connection with your provision of Direct Patient Care and otherwise is in compliance with the Conformance Standards. You will not modify any records that you retain in accordance with this section other than as may be expressly authorized in the Conformance Standards. For clarity, you may annotate a discrete record provided that the discrete record is not itself modified other than as expressly authorized in the Conformance Standards.
  2. Use of Retained Records. You may use any records retained by you in accordance with section 6(a) of this Agreement for a purpose authorized under section 24(1) of the Act, including for the purpose of monitoring your own Practice as described in s. 16(1) of the IM Regulation.
  3. Disclosure to Third Parties. You will not, and will ensure that Supervised Persons do not, disclose PharmaNet Data to any Unauthorized Person, unless disclosure is required for Direct Patient Care or is otherwise authorized under section 24(1) of the Act.
  4. No Disclosure for Market Research. You will not, and will ensure that Supervised Persons do not, disclose PharmaNet Data for the purpose of market research.
  5. Responding to Patient Access Requests. Aside from any records retained by you in accordance with section 6(a) of this Agreement, you will not provide to patients any copies of records containing PharmaNet Data or “print outs” produced directly from PharmaNet, and will refer any requests for access to such records or “print outs” to the Province.
  6. Legal Demands for records contained in PharmaNet. You will immediately notify the Province if you receive any order, demand or request compelling, or threatening to compel, disclosure of records contained in PharmaNet. You will cooperate and consult with the Province in responding to any such demands. For greater certainty, the foregoing requires that you notify the Province only with respect to any access requests or demands for records contained in PharmaNet, and not records retained by you in accordance with section 6(a) of this Agreement.
7. ACCURACY

You will make reasonable efforts to ensure that any Personal Information recorded by you or a Supervised Person in PharmaNet is accurate, complete and up to date. In the event that you become aware of a material inaccuracy or error in such information, you will take reasonable steps to investigate the inaccuracy or error, correct it if necessary, and notify the Province of the inaccuracy or error and any steps taken.
